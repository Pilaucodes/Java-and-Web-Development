    Manual Therapy: used to treat musculoskeletal pain and disability.
    It includes manipulation, mobilization, massage,and various
    soft tissue techniques, all aimed at reducing pain and muscle
    tension while improving range of motion and circulation. It is
    effective for treating conditions such as back pain, joint pain,
    headaches, and various musculoskeletal disorders.

    Geriatric Therapy: is specifically tailored for older adults
    for age-related conditions. This therapy includes strengt training,
    balance exercises, and mobility training, all aimed at improving
    overall physical function and independence in elderly patients.
    It is important for reducing the risk of falls and managing chronic
    pain associated with conditions like arthritis and osteoporosis.
    Each of these therapiesis essential in rehabilitation and enhancing
    the quality of life for people with specific health conditions.

    Sports therapy: a specialized field dedicated to the prevention,
    assessment,and rehabilitation of sports-related injuries. It involves
    physical rehabilitation,strength training,and injury prevention
    strategies, which not only enhance athletic performance but also
    reduce the risk of injury and facilitate faster recovery from injuries.
    Common conditions treated in sports therapy includes sprains, strains,
    tendonitis, and both acute and chronic sports injuries.

    Neurological therapy: focuses on treating patients with
    neurological conditions that affect movement and function.
    This therapy uses techniques like neurodevelopmental methods,
    constraint-induced movement therapy, and functional training to
    help improve motor control and coordination.The benefits of
    neurological therapy include enhanced balance and mobility, aiding
    recovery from strokes,traumatic brain injuries,and diseases such as
    multiple sclerosis and Parkinson's.
